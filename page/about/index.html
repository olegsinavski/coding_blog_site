Hello, thats me:

Some dream that robots are going to be helping us around.

I’m an experienced researcher, developer and manager working in autonomous navigation trying to make it a reality.

RL and Self-driving

Currently, I’m a Principal Applied Scientist at Wayve working on end-to-end self-driving systems.

Some areas I’m currently investigating:

  • Model Based RL like MCTS and adaptive planning
  • Inverse and Offline RL
  • Multiagent RL
  • Novel View Point Synthesis and self-driving evaluation

I’m also in technical leadership plus a bit of management.

Scalable Robotics and management

Previously, I held a title of VP of Research and Development at BrainCorp. I built a research team from ground up, scaled up AI, robotics and machine learning systems from zero to thousands of robots deployed across the world bringing the company 100M+$ of investments and revenue (longer story).

We had a chance to work on:

  • Motion planning, optimization and search algorithms (paper)
  • SLAM (pose graphs, particle filters etc)
  • Perception (DL, computer vision, depth and lidars - talk)
  • Large scale data analysis and ML

Ages ago I was part of a Robotic Soccer team and went to a few international championships.

Real and artificial brains

Before pivoting into robotics, BrainCorp was a neuroscience company where I worked as a Senior Research Scientist. I worked on simulated human and animal brains and other fun stuff:

  • Spiking neurons, brain modeling
  • RL, Supervised and Unsupervised learning in biologically-plausible neurons
  • Neuromorphic hardware and energy efficient computation

Ah, also I’m Computational Neuroscience PhD from a robotic department of MPEI. I’m an author of 40+ patents (just search here for Oleg Sinyavskiy).

A lot of coding

I am a coder since I can remember myself, so I qualify to write a coding blog :)

Several times I created company-wide design and coding guidelines. I started in C++ working on city-scale licence plate detection systems and telepresence robots. Then I switched to Python and designed software that powers tens of thousands of systems across the world.
